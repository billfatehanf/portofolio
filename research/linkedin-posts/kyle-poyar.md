## Post 1: From VC Partner to Solopreneur — My LinkedIn Story

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Personal Brand, Career Transition

In 2024, I left OpenView Venture Partners after 8 years.

I went from "Kyle Poyar, Operating Partner at OpenView" to "Kyle Poyar, creator of Growth Unhinged."

My LinkedIn following went UP, not down.

Here's what I learned about personal brand vs corporate brand:

1. The followers were mine, not OpenView's
2. People followed me for my PERSPECTIVE, not my title
3. When I left, my content still resonated because it was still ME
4. My newsletter (Growth Unhinged) transferred seamlessly

The lesson for everyone building on LinkedIn:
→ Your personal brand travels with you
→ Your company's brand stays behind
→ Invest in YOUR voice, not just your logo

If you're an executive at a SaaS company, start building your personal brand NOW. You'll need it eventually.

**Key Takeaway:** Personal brand on LinkedIn is portable; corporate brand is not. Executives should invest in their own voice and audience because it follows them across career transitions.

## Post 2: The Death of Seat-Based Pricing

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Pricing Strategy, B2B SaaS

Seat-based pricing is dying. Here's the data.

In our 2025 Monetization Survey (240+ software companies):
- 45% of AI-native companies use consumption or outcome-based pricing
- Only 22% of AI-native companies use seat-based pricing
- Hybrid models (seat + usage) grew 30% year-over-year

Why seats are dying:
1. AI does work that "seats" used to represent
2. Customers resist paying per-seat when AI reduces headcount
3. Usage-based pricing better aligns cost with value
4. Buyers want to pay for OUTCOMES, not access

What this means for your LinkedIn content:

If you're a SaaS company, your pricing model IS a content topic.

Post about:
- How you think about pricing
- Why you chose your model
- How customers benefit from it
- The trade-offs you considered

Pricing transparency builds trust. And trust drives pipeline.

**Key Takeaway:** Seat-based pricing is declining, replaced by hybrid and consumption models. Pricing philosophy is a powerful LinkedIn content topic that builds trust and transparency.

## Post 3: Why Data-Driven Content Wins on LinkedIn

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Content Strategy, Data

The #1 reason my LinkedIn posts perform well: DATA.

Not opinions. Not takes. DATA.

When I share:
"45% of AI-native companies use consumption-based pricing"
vs.
"I think more companies should use consumption-based pricing"

The first gets 3x more engagement. Every time.

Why data wins:
1. It's shareable (people screenshot and share data)
2. It's credible (you did the work)
3. It's differentiated (anyone can have an opinion; data is harder)
4. It's useful (people use your data in their presentations and pitches)

How to create data-driven LinkedIn content:
→ Survey your customers or community
→ Analyze public benchmarks with a unique lens
→ Share proprietary metrics (with appropriate context)
→ Create simple charts/visualizations

You don't need a research team. You need a Google Form and 50 responses.

**Key Takeaway:** Data-driven LinkedIn content consistently outperforms opinion-based content. It's more shareable, credible, and useful. Even simple surveys can generate powerful data for posts.

## Post 4: The Newsletter-to-LinkedIn Pipeline

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Content Distribution, Newsletter Strategy

Growth Unhinged (my newsletter) has 50k+ subscribers.

Here's my content pipeline:

1. I write one deep-dive newsletter per week (1,500-2,500 words)
2. I pull 3-5 key insights from the newsletter
3. Each insight becomes a standalone LinkedIn post
4. The LinkedIn posts drive newsletter subscriptions
5. The newsletter drives deeper engagement and trust

This is a LOOP, not a funnel.

LinkedIn → Newsletter → LinkedIn → Newsletter

Each channel feeds the other.

If you're building thought leadership on LinkedIn, pair it with a newsletter. LinkedIn gives you reach. Your newsletter gives you a direct relationship.

Don't build your entire audience on rented land (LinkedIn). Own the relationship through email.

**Key Takeaway:** A newsletter-to-LinkedIn pipeline creates a self-reinforcing loop. LinkedIn provides reach and discovery; the newsletter provides depth and ownership of the audience relationship.

## Post 5: The PLG Paradox — Free Users vs Paying Customers

**Date:** January 2026
**Theme:** Product-Led Growth, Monetization

The PLG paradox that nobody talks about:

More free users ≠ more revenue.

I've seen companies with:
- 100,000 free users and $2M ARR
- 5,000 free users and $20M ARR

The difference? Conversion architecture.

The companies winning at PLG in 2026:
→ Are highly opinionated about who their free plan is FOR
→ Build friction at the right moments (not the wrong ones)
→ Use product data to identify expansion-ready accounts
→ Pair PLG with targeted sales motions (PLS)

LinkedIn content lesson: if you're PLG, stop bragging about "signups."

Start sharing:
- How you think about conversion
- What your free-to-paid journey looks like
- Why you chose your pricing tiers
- Real metrics on what works and what doesn't

Transparency about your business model is some of the best-performing B2B content on LinkedIn.

**Key Takeaway:** PLG success is about conversion architecture, not volume of free users. Sharing transparent insights about your business model and pricing decisions generates high-performing LinkedIn content.
