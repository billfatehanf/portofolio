## Post 1: The Death of MQLs

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Demand Generation, Metrics

Hot take: MQLs are the worst metric in B2B marketing.

Here's why most companies are stuck:

They gate a piece of content behind a form.
Someone downloads it.
Marketing says "We generated a lead!"
Sales calls the person.
The person says "I just wanted the PDF."
Sales marks it as "unqualified."

Rinse. Repeat. Waste money.

What we should be doing instead:
→ Create ungated content that builds real awareness
→ Measure "dark social" — where people actually hear about you
→ Ask prospects "How did you hear about us?" on demo forms
→ Trust that brand-building drives pipeline over time

The companies winning in 2026 don't count MQLs. They count pipeline influenced by brand.

**Key Takeaway:** Gated content and MQLs create a false sense of progress. Demand generation — building brand awareness through ungated, high-value content — is what actually drives pipeline.

## Post 2: Why Your LinkedIn Company Page Gets Zero Engagement

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Personal Brand vs Company Brand

Your LinkedIn company page has 25k followers.
Your CEO's personal profile has 3k followers.

Yet your CEO's posts get 10x more engagement.

Why?

Because people follow people, not logos.

The biggest mistake in B2B marketing is treating LinkedIn like a corporate PR channel.

Here's what the best B2B companies do:
1. CEO and leadership team post from personal accounts
2. They share opinions, stories, and real experiences
3. They link back to company when relevant (but rarely)
4. The company page reposts employee content (not the other way around)

Stop investing in your company page. Start investing in your executives' voices.

**Key Takeaway:** Personal profiles consistently outperform company pages on LinkedIn. B2B companies should invest in executive thought leadership, not corporate content.

## Post 3: The Podcast-to-LinkedIn Pipeline

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Content Repurposing, Multi-Channel

I've helped 50+ B2B SaaS companies build their content engines.

The best-performing strategy isn't what you'd expect:

It's a weekly podcast → LinkedIn content pipeline.

Here's the playbook:
1. Record a 30-min podcast episode weekly
2. Pull 5-8 short clips (60-90 seconds each)
3. Post them as native video on LinkedIn
4. Write 3-5 text posts based on the discussion
5. That's 8-13 pieces of content from ONE conversation

Why this works:
- Podcasts capture authentic, unscripted insights
- Video performs extremely well on LinkedIn right now
- Text posts expand on ideas for those who don't watch video
- You build relationships with podcast guests (cross-pollination)

This is the most efficient content engine I've seen in B2B.

**Key Takeaway:** A weekly podcast can fuel an entire LinkedIn content strategy. Record once, repurpose into video clips and text posts for maximum efficiency and authenticity.

## Post 4: Stop Measuring What Doesn't Matter

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Attribution, Marketing Metrics

Question for every B2B CMO:

If you turned off all your "lead gen" programs tomorrow — gated content, webinars with 500 "registrants," ebook downloads — what would happen to your actual pipeline?

I've seen dozens of companies do this experiment.

The result every single time: pipeline stays the same or goes UP.

Because those leads were never real pipeline anyway.

What actually drives pipeline in B2B SaaS:
→ Word of mouth from customers
→ Organic LinkedIn content from your team
→ Podcast appearances and guesting
→ Community participation
→ Being genuinely helpful in public

None of these have a neat attribution model. That's the point.

**Key Takeaway:** Most "measurable" B2B marketing activities (gated content, webinars) generate vanity metrics, not pipeline. The activities that truly drive revenue are harder to measure but far more effective.

## Post 5: The "Dark Social" Thesis

**Date:** January 2026
**Theme:** Attribution, Buyer Behavior

Where do B2B buyers actually discover new software?

Not from your Google ads.
Not from your gated ebook.
Not from your cold email.

They discover you in "dark social":
- A Slack channel where someone recommended you
- A LinkedIn post from a colleague
- A podcast episode during their commute
- A conversation at an industry dinner
- A friend's text message

None of this shows up in your attribution model.
All of it shows up in your pipeline.

This is why I ask EVERY demo-booked prospect: "How did you first hear about us?"

The #1 answer, consistently: "I saw your content on LinkedIn."

Build your brand. Trust the process. The pipeline follows.

**Key Takeaway:** Most B2B buying decisions are influenced by "dark social" — channels that don't show up in attribution models. LinkedIn organic content is consistently the #1 source when companies ask prospects directly.
