## Post 3: Be Intentional, Not "Brilliant"

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Content Strategy, Consistency

The biggest trap in B2B content marketing: trying to be brilliant.

"We need to create viral content."
"We need to say something nobody's ever said."
"We need a groundbreaking take."

This leads to: analysis paralysis. You post nothing.

Instead, be INTENTIONAL.

Intentional means:
→ Pick ONE clear point of view
→ Post about it consistently (3-5x per week)
→ Say the same thing in different ways
→ Back it up with stories, data, and examples
→ Repeat for 12 months

Jay Baer said it best: "Don't be afraid to say the same thing 1,000 times. Your audience isn't as tired of it as you are."

Consistency > brilliance. Every time.

**Key Takeaway:** Chasing "brilliant" or viral content leads to paralysis. Being intentional — consistently posting one clear point of view in different formats — builds authority over time.

## Post 4: The Problem-Market Fit Test

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Startup Strategy, Market Validation

Before product-market fit, there's PROBLEM-market fit.

Most founders build a product and then look for a problem it solves. This is backwards.

Problem-market fit means: the problem you're solving is felt URGENTLY by a specific, reachable group of people.

How to test problem-market fit on LinkedIn:

1. Post about the PROBLEM (not your solution) 3-5 times
2. Measure: Do people in your target audience engage?
3. Read comments: Do they use the same language you used?
4. DM engaged people: "Tell me more about this challenge"
5. If you get 50+ conversations, you have problem-market fit

LinkedIn is the cheapest, fastest way to validate problem-market fit.

No landing pages. No ads. No product needed. Just posts about the problem.

**Key Takeaway:** LinkedIn is the cheapest, fastest tool for validating problem-market fit. Post about the problem (not your solution) and measure engagement to gauge market urgency.

## Post 5: Why Community Is the Future of GTM

**Date:** January 2026
**Theme:** Community-Led Growth, B2B Strategy

Prediction: by 2027, every top-performing B2B SaaS company will have a community strategy.

Not a "customer community" buried on your website.

A real community where your ICP gathers, learns, and connects.

Why community matters for GTM:
1. It creates word-of-mouth at scale
2. It gives you direct access to customer insights
3. It reduces churn (people don't leave communities they rely on)
4. It generates organic content (members create content FOR you)
5. It shortens sales cycles (prospects pre-educate themselves)

And LinkedIn is the gateway.

Your LinkedIn content attracts people. Your community retains them.

LinkedIn = top of funnel
Community = middle of funnel
Product = bottom of funnel

Build the full stack. Start with LinkedIn.

**Key Takeaway:** Community is becoming essential to B2B GTM strategy. LinkedIn serves as the top-of-funnel attraction engine, while community retains and nurtures prospects through the buying journey.
