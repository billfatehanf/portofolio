## Post 1: Stop Pitch-Slapping on LinkedIn

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Social Selling, Outreach

If your LinkedIn strategy is "connect → immediately pitch," you're doing it wrong.

I call it "pitch-slapping" — and it's killing your pipeline.

Here's what happens:
1. You send a connection request
2. The person accepts
3. You immediately send a 3-paragraph pitch
4. They either ignore you or disconnect

Instead, try the "Content-First" approach:
1. Create 3-5 posts per week sharing your expertise
2. Engage with your prospect's posts for 2-3 weeks
3. Send a connection request referencing their content
4. Start a conversation (not a pitch)
5. Offer value before asking for anything

This works because:
→ They've seen your name in their feed
→ They know you're knowledgeable
→ They feel like they already know you
→ The conversation feels natural, not forced

Social selling = building relationships through content. Not pitch-slapping.

**Key Takeaway:** "Pitch-slapping" (connecting then immediately pitching) destroys trust. The content-first approach — building visibility through posts and engagement before outreach — creates warm, natural conversations.

## Post 2: The Anti-Automation Thesis

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Sales Strategy, AI

Unpopular opinion: most sales teams should STOP automating their outreach.

"But Morgan, automation saves time!"

Sure. It saves time by sending bad messages at scale.

If your messaging is bad, automation just means you're burning your market FASTER.

Here's my framework:

1. FIRST: manually test your messaging with 50 prospects
2. Get replies? Great. Now you have a template that works.
3. THEN (and only then) add light automation

The companies I work with at AMP Creative follow this rule:
- Manual first → validate messaging
- Semi-automated second → scale what works
- Fully automated never → always keep the human touch

The best prospecting on LinkedIn is still PERSONAL.
A custom voice note beats 100 automated InMails.

**Key Takeaway:** Automation without validated messaging just scales bad outreach faster. Test manually first, then scale what works while keeping the human touch — especially on LinkedIn.

## Post 3: LinkedIn Video Is Your Secret Weapon

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Video Content, LinkedIn Algorithm

If you're in B2B sales and not creating LinkedIn video, you're missing the biggest opportunity of 2026.

Why video works on LinkedIn:
1. 95% of creators DON'T do video (less competition)
2. LinkedIn's algorithm is boosting native video right now
3. Video builds trust 10x faster than text
4. Prospects feel like they "know" you before you ever DM them

My formula for LinkedIn video:
→ 30-60 seconds MAX
→ One specific insight per video
→ No fancy editing (authenticity wins)
→ Direct-to-camera (build personal connection)
→ Include captions (80% watch without sound)

I went from 0 to 160k followers largely because of video.

You don't need a studio. You need a phone and one useful idea.

**Key Takeaway:** Short-form (30-60s) native LinkedIn video is massively under-utilized in B2B. It builds trust faster than text, gets algorithm boost, and requires zero production value — just authenticity and one clear insight.

## Post 4: The "Comment Magnet" Framework

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Engagement Strategy, Content Frameworks

You don't need to post every day to grow on LinkedIn.

You need to post content that drives COMMENTS.

Comments > Likes > Shares in LinkedIn's algorithm.

My "Comment Magnet" framework:

1. **Ask a specific question:** "What's the worst cold email you've ever received?"
2. **Share a contrarian take:** "Cold calling isn't dead — your script is."
3. **Use fill-in-the-blank:** "The biggest mistake in sales prospecting is ______."
4. **Show your work + ask for feedback:** "Here's my new outreach script. What would you change?"
5. **Create a debate:** "Video > text for LinkedIn prospecting. Agree or disagree?"

Each of these invites the reader to PARTICIPATE, not just consume.

One "comment magnet" post per week can drive more growth than 5 regular posts.

**Key Takeaway:** Comments are the most valuable engagement signal on LinkedIn. The "comment magnet" framework (questions, debates, fill-in-the-blanks) drives comments and algorithmic reach without requiring daily posting.

## Post 5: Multi-Channel Social Selling

**Date:** January 2026
**Theme:** Prospecting, Multi-Channel Strategy

The days of single-channel prospecting are over.

LinkedIn DM alone? Not enough.
Cold email alone? Definitely not enough.
Cold call alone? Good luck.

The modern B2B prospecting stack:

Day 1: Like + comment on prospect's LinkedIn post
Day 3: Connect + personalized note referencing their content
Day 5: Send a 30-second video voice note on LinkedIn
Day 7: Follow up with a cold email referencing the LinkedIn conversation
Day 10: Call them (they'll recognize your name)

By Day 10, you've appeared in their world 5 times. You're not a stranger anymore.

This is what "social selling" actually means. Not just posting on LinkedIn — but using LinkedIn as ONE layer in a multi-channel approach.

**Key Takeaway:** Effective B2B prospecting requires a multi-channel sequence where LinkedIn engagement, DMs, email, and calling work together. Each touchpoint builds familiarity, making subsequent outreach warm rather than cold.
