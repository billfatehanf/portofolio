## Post 1: The Content Repurposing System

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Content Strategy, Efficiency

I've posted on LinkedIn every single day for 4 years.

But here's the dirty secret: I don't create new content every day.

I use a system I call "Think Once, Publish 10 Times."

Here's how it works:

1. I write one deep newsletter essay per week
2. I pull 5–7 key ideas from that essay
3. Each idea becomes its own LinkedIn post
4. I vary the format: list, story, observation, contrarian take
5. I schedule them across the week

The result? 5–7 posts per week from ~2 hours of deep thinking.

Stop trying to be original every day. Be systematic instead.

**Key Takeaway:** Repurposing > creating from scratch. Build systems that compound your best thinking across multiple formats.

## Post 2: Why Most LinkedIn Profiles Fail

**Date:** March 2026
**Theme:** Profile Optimization, Personal Branding

Your LinkedIn profile is not a resume. It's a landing page.

Yet 95% of profiles still read like:
- "Experienced professional with 15+ years..."
- "Passionate about helping businesses grow..."
- "Results-driven leader with a track record..."

None of this tells me what problem you solve.

Here's the formula I use:
→ WHO I help (specific audience)
→ WHAT I help them do (transformation)
→ HOW I do it (mechanism)

Example: "I help solopreneurs build $1M+ businesses using LinkedIn and email — no ads, no employees, no funding."

Rewrite your headline today. Be specific. Be clear. Be useful.

**Key Takeaway:** Treat your LinkedIn profile as a value proposition, not a biography. Clarity of "who + what + how" drives conversions.

## Post 3: The "Spiky Point of View" Framework

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Content Differentiation, Thought Leadership

Want to stand out on LinkedIn? Stop sharing "tips."

Instead, develop what I call a "Spiky Point of View."

This is an opinion so clear, so specific, and so bold that people either love it or hate it.

Examples:
- "You don't need a team to build a $5M business"
- "Cold outreach is dead for 95% of B2B companies"
- "LinkedIn carousels are overrated — text-only posts win"

A spiky POV does three things:
1. Attracts your ideal audience instantly
2. Repels people who aren't a fit (that's good)
3. Makes you memorable in a sea of generic content

Be polarizing. Be specific. Be yourself.

**Key Takeaway:** Generic content gets ignored. A "spiky point of view" — an opinion that polarizes — is the fastest way to build authority and attract the right audience.

## Post 4: The LinkedIn Engagement Hack Nobody Talks About

**Date:** February 2026
**Theme:** Growth Strategy, Engagement

I grew from 0 to 500k followers in 3.5 years.

The #1 tactic nobody talks about: strategic commenting.

Here's my "Draft Off Giants" method:

1. Identify 10 creators in your niche with 50k+ followers
2. Turn on notifications for each of them
3. Within 15 min of them posting, leave a thoughtful comment
4. Not "Great post!" — a genuine 2-3 sentence addition
5. Do this every day for 90 days

Why it works:
- Their audience sees YOUR name and expertise
- The algorithm rewards active engagement
- You build real relationships with influential creators

I attribute at least 40% of my early growth to this strategy.

**Key Takeaway:** Commenting on others' posts — especially within the first 15 minutes — is one of the most underrated growth strategies on LinkedIn.

## Post 5: Why I Never Use Links in LinkedIn Posts

**Date:** January 2026
**Theme:** Algorithm Strategy, Zero-Click Content

I stopped putting links in my LinkedIn posts 3 years ago.

Here's why:

LinkedIn's algorithm penalizes external links. It wants people to STAY on the platform.

When you include a link:
→ Reach drops 40-50%
→ Fewer eyeballs on your expertise
→ Less engagement = less growth

My alternative:
1. Give the FULL value in the post itself
2. If I mention a resource, I say "link in first comment"
3. Or I say "Comment 'LINK' and I'll DM you"

The last approach is genius because:
- It drives engagement (algorithm loves comments)
- It creates a DM conversation (relationship building)
- It captures demand (you know who's interested)

Zero-click content wins. Every time.

**Key Takeaway:** LinkedIn penalizes posts with external links. Provide full value within the post, then use "link in comments" or "DM me" to drive action without sacrificing reach.
