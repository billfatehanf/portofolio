## Video 1: "AI Is Breaking Every Pricing Model" (Product Growth Podcast)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Channel** | Product Growth Podcast |
| **URL** | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kylepoyar-ai-pricing-models |
| **Date** | September 2025 |
| **Duration** | ~42 min |
| **Speaker** | Kyle Poyar |

### Transcript Summary

Poyar opens with data from his research at Growth Unhinged:

"We surveyed 240+ software and AI companies for our 2025 Monetization Report. The headline finding: seat-based pricing is declining rapidly. Only 22% of AI-native companies use per-seat pricing. Compare that to traditional SaaS, where it's still above 60%."

**On why seats are dying:**
"Think about it from the buyer's perspective. If I'm buying an AI tool that automates what 3 people used to do, why would I pay PER SEAT? The whole point is that fewer people need to use the tool. Seat-based pricing creates a perverse incentive: the better the AI works, the fewer seats you need, and the LESS the vendor gets paid."

"That's a broken model. And buyers know it."

**On the rise of hybrid models:**
"What's replacing seats? Hybrid models. A base subscription fee plus usage-based components. We're seeing this across the board:"

"- HubSpot added credit-based pricing for AI features
- Salesforce launched AI credits alongside its seat licenses
- Figma uses a credit model for AI generation features"

"The credit model is elegant because it gives customers predictability (they buy X credits upfront) while giving vendors usage-based upside. It's the best of both worlds."

**On pricing as LinkedIn content:**
"Here's something most founders miss: your pricing model is FASCINATING content for LinkedIn. People love reading about pricing decisions."

"When I post about pricing data on LinkedIn — like 'Here's how 240+ companies price AI features' — it gets 3x more engagement than generic growth advice. Why? Because:"

"1. Data is shareable — people screenshot it and send it to their VP Finance
2. Pricing affects everyone — VPs, founders, PMs, CFOs all care
3. It's novel — most people keep pricing decisions private
4. It positions you as a trusted expert — you did original research"

"If you're a SaaS founder, post about your pricing journey. Why you chose your model. What you experimented with. What failed. This transparency builds enormous trust."

### Key Themes
- Seat-based pricing is declining (only 22% of AI-native companies use it)
- Hybrid models (subscription + usage/credits) are the dominant trend
- Credit-based pricing provides predictability for customers + usage upside for vendors
- Pricing is a high-performing LinkedIn content topic — data-driven pricing posts get 3x engagement

## Video 2: "From VC Partner to Solopreneur: Building in Public on LinkedIn" (Mostly Growth Podcast)

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Channel** | Mostly Growth (Kyle Poyar & CJ Gustafson) |
| **URL** | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kylepoyar-mostly-growth-solopreneur |
| **Date** | March 2026 |
| **Duration** | ~35 min |
| **Speaker** | Kyle Poyar, CJ Gustafson |

### Transcript Summary

Poyar discusses his transition from VC operating partner to solopreneur:

"In November 2024, I left OpenView after 8 years. It was the hardest professional decision of my life. But I had a thesis: the personal brand I'd built on LinkedIn was more valuable than any corporate title."

**On personal brand portability:**
"When I was at OpenView, I had the credibility of a top VC firm behind me. But here's what surprised me: after I left, my LinkedIn engagement went UP. My newsletter subscribers grew FASTER."

"Why? Because people weren't following me for OpenView. They were following me for MY analysis, MY data, MY frameworks. The brand was personal, not corporate."

"This is the most important lesson for anyone at a company right now: your personal brand is PORTABLE. Your corporate brand is NOT. Invest in both. But know that only one follows you when you leave."

**On building Growth Unhinged:**
"Growth Unhinged went from a side project at OpenView to my full-time business. It now has 50,000+ subscribers. Here's how I built it:"

"1. I started by posting data-driven insights on LinkedIn — specific numbers, benchmarks, survey results
2. Those posts drove newsletter subscriptions — I'd say 'deep dive in this week's newsletter, link in comments'
3. The newsletter built deeper trust — 1,500-2,500 words going deep on one topic
4. Trust converted to consulting and advisory revenue"

"The pipeline is: LinkedIn post (discovery) → Newsletter (depth) → Consulting (revenue). LinkedIn is the top of the funnel. The newsletter is the middle. Consulting is the bottom."

**On data as a content moat:**
"Anyone can share opinions on LinkedIn. Very few people share ORIGINAL DATA. That's my moat."

"Every quarter, I run surveys: How are companies pricing AI features? What monetization models are working? What's the state of PLG? I compile the results and publish them — first on LinkedIn (headline stats), then in the newsletter (full analysis)."

"This creates content that cannot be copied by AI. ChatGPT can write '5 tips for SaaS pricing.' It CANNOT write 'our survey of 240 companies found that 45% of AI-native companies use consumption-based pricing, up from 31% last year.' That's proprietary data. That's a moat."

"My advice to anyone building thought leadership on LinkedIn: find a way to generate ORIGINAL data. Survey your community. Analyze public datasets with a unique lens. Benchmark your own performance and share the results. Data is the ultimate differentiator."

### Key Themes
- Personal brand is portable across career transitions; corporate brand is not
- LinkedIn → newsletter → consulting is a complete solopreneur business pipeline
- Original data (surveys, benchmarks) creates an uncopiable content moat
- Data-driven LinkedIn posts get 3x more engagement than opinion-based content
- 50,000+ newsletter subscribers built entirely through LinkedIn organic distribution
