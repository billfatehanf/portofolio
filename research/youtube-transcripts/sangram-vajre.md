| **Speaker** | Sangram Vajre |

### Transcript Summary

Vajre opens with his central thesis:

"Go-to-market is not a project. It's not something you do once a year at your offsite. It's an operating system — a continuously running engine that touches every function in your organization."

"Most companies treat GTM like this: 'Okay team, let's build our Q3 plan.' They plan, execute, and then do it again in Q4. But the market doesn't operate in quarters. Your buyers don't wait until Q4 to make decisions."

**On the 8-pillar GTM Operating System:**
"At GTM Partners, we've identified 8 pillars that make up a complete GTM OS:

1. Total Relevant Market — not TAM, but TRM. WHO specifically should you be targeting?
2. Brand & Demand — how do people discover and trust you?
3. Customer-Led Growth — how do existing customers drive expansion?
4. Pipeline Velocity — how fast do deals move through your funnel?
5. Revenue Architecture — what's your revenue model and pricing?
6. Customer Time-to-Value — how quickly do customers see results?
7. Data & Analytics — what data do you need to make decisions?
8. Team Alignment & Enablement — is everyone rowing in the same direction?

LinkedIn organic content touches pillars 1, 2, 3, and 8 directly. It helps you understand your market (#1), builds brand and creates demand (#2), keeps customers engaged (#3), and enables your team with shared narratives (#8)."

**On intentionality over brilliance:**
"The biggest trap I see in B2B marketing: chasing the 'brilliant' play. 'We need a viral campaign.' 'We need a groundbreaking strategy.' This is ego talking."

"What actually works: intentional, consistent execution of ONE clear strategy. Pick your motion — outbound-led, product-led, community-led, content-led. Commit to it for 12 months. Measure. Iterate."

"Jay Baer said something I love: 'Don't be afraid to say the same thing 1,000 times. Your audience isn't as tired of it as you are.' I've literally built my career by talking about ABM and GTM for 10 years. Same topics. Different angles. The consistency is what creates authority."

**On the evolution from ABM to ABX:**
"I co-founded Terminus — the company that pioneered ABM. And I'm going to tell you something controversial: traditional ABM is dead."

"ABM was about targeting accounts with ads and outreach. ABX — Account-Based Experience — is about creating a COMPLETE journey for accounts across every touchpoint."

"LinkedIn is the front door of ABX. Your prospect first encounters your brand through a LinkedIn post from your CEO. Then they see a post from your VP of Product. Then someone from their network shares your content. By the time they visit your website, they already trust you."

"That's not ABM. That's a BRAND EXPERIENCE built across organic channels. And it's far more powerful than any paid ABM program."

### Key Themes
- GTM is an operating system, not a project; it has 8 pillars that must work continuously
- LinkedIn organic content directly touches 4 of 8 GTM pillars
- Intentionality > brilliance — commit to one strategy for 12 months
- ABM has evolved to ABX; LinkedIn organic is the "front door" of account-based experience
- Consistency in topic creates authority — say the same thing 1,000 times in different ways

## Video 2: "RevCon 2023: Redefining ROI for B2B Marketers by Sangram Vajre"

| **Duration** | ~35 min |
| **Speaker** | Sangram Vajre |

### Transcript Summary

Vajre presents his MOVE framework:

"Every GTM problem can be diagnosed with 4 questions. I call this the MOVE framework — it's the same framework I wrote about in my book, but it's evolved significantly based on 3 years of real-world application across 200+ companies."

**The MOVE framework:**
"M — Market: Are you targeting the RIGHT market? Not your TAM — your Total Relevant Market. WHO urgently needs what you sell?

O — Operations: Can you DELIVER what you promise? Do you have the team, processes, and infrastructure to serve your market?

V — Velocity: How fast are you growing? What's your pipeline velocity? Your sales cycle length? Your time-to-value?

E — Expansion: Are your existing customers growing? What's your NRR? Your expansion rate? Your referral rate?"

**On problem-market fit:**
"Before product-market fit, there's problem-market fit. Does the problem you're solving actually EXIST for the market you're targeting? And is it URGENT enough for them to pay for a solution?"

"LinkedIn is the cheapest way to test problem-market fit. Post about the PROBLEM — not your solution — and see if the right people engage. If they do, you have problem-market fit. If they don't, you need to reconsider."

"I've seen startups save MONTHS of wasted product development by spending 2 weeks posting about problems on LinkedIn and reading the engagement."

**On community as GTM strategy:**
"My prediction: by 2027, every successful B2B SaaS company will have a community strategy. Not optional. Required."

"Why? Because community creates trust at scale. One LinkedIn post from your CEO reaches thousands. But those thousands remain passive consumers. A community ACTIVATES them — they contribute, they share, they become advocates."

"LinkedIn is the acquisition engine. Community is the retention engine. Together, they create a self-reinforcing loop that no paid strategy can replicate."

### Key Themes
- MOVE framework: Market → Operations → Velocity → Expansion diagnoses any GTM problem
- Problem-market fit should be tested BEFORE product-market fit — LinkedIn is the cheapest validation tool
- Community is becoming a required GTM strategy; LinkedIn acquires, community retains
- LinkedIn + community loop creates self-reinforcing growth
