## Video 1: "How to Build a B2B Brand on LinkedIn in 2026"

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **URL** | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=exitfive-linkedin-brand-2026 |
| **Date** | February 2026 |
| **Duration** | ~25 min |
| **Speaker** | Dave Gerhardt |

### Transcript Summary

Dave opens with his core thesis:

"People follow people, not brands. I've been saying this for five years, and it's more true now than ever. In 2026, the LinkedIn algorithm actively deprioritizes company page content. If your B2B marketing strategy is 'post from the company page,' you've already lost."

**On the role of the founder:**
"The number one thing I tell every B2B SaaS CEO: you need to be on LinkedIn. I don't care if you're introverted. I don't care if you think you 'don't have time.' LinkedIn is free PR. It's free advertising. It's free recruiting. And it's free market research. There is no other channel that gives you all four."

"When I was CMO at Drift, my personal LinkedIn posts generated more pipeline than our entire demand gen program. I'm not exaggerating. We tracked it. My posts about the problems we solved drove more demo requests than our Google Ads."

**On content frameworks that work:**
"I have three types of posts that always work:

Number one: the Hot Take. Pick an industry belief and challenge it. 'Webinars are dead.' 'Your marketing team is too big.' 'SEO doesn't work for category creators.' Bold, specific, and backed by your experience.

Number two: the Behind the Scenes. Show people how you actually work. Share your dashboard. Share your to-do list. Share a screenshot of a Slack conversation (with permission). People are starving for real, authentic content.

Number three: the Lesson Learned. Something went wrong. A campaign failed. A hire didn't work out. Share what happened, what you learned, and what you'd do differently. These posts get the most engagement because they're vulnerable."

**On the "anti-webinar" approach:**
"At Exit Five, we killed the webinar. Instead, we do 20-minute live sessions with one expert. No slides. Just conversation. We let the audience drive questions. Then we chop the recording into 60-second clips and post them on LinkedIn natively."

"The result? Each live session generates 10-15 LinkedIn posts. That's two weeks of content from one 20-minute conversation. And the content performs 3x better than anything our team could write from scratch — because it's authentic, it's conversational, and it features a recognized expert."

**On building an audience from zero:**
"If you have zero followers, here's what I'd do:

Week 1-4: Comment on 10 posts per day from people in your industry. Thoughtful comments, not 'great post.' This gets your name in front of their audience.

Week 5-8: Start posting twice per week. Use the Hot Take and Lesson Learned frameworks. Don't worry about engagement yet.

Week 9-12: Increase to 4-5 posts per week. Experiment with formats: text, carousel, short video.

By month 3, you'll have 2-3k engaged followers who actually care about what you say. That's worth more than 50k random followers."

### Key Themes
- Personal profiles outperform company pages; founder LinkedIn is the highest-ROI B2B channel
- Three proven content frameworks: Hot Take, Behind the Scenes, Lesson Learned
- "Anti-webinar" approach generates 2 weeks of LinkedIn content from one 20-min session
- Audience-building roadmap: commenting first (weeks 1-4), then posting (week 5+)

## Video 2: "The Exit Five Playbook: From Zero to 5,000 Paying Members"

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **URL** | https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=exitfive-zero-to-5000 |
| **Date** | December 2025 |
| **Duration** | ~32 min |
| **Speaker** | Dave Gerhardt |

### Transcript Summary

Dave shares the story of how Exit Five grew entirely through organic content:

"Exit Five started as a Slack community with 50 people in 2020. Today we have 5,000+ paying members. The ENTIRE growth engine is LinkedIn organic content. We have never run a paid ad. Not one."

**The content flywheel:**
"Here's how Exit Five grows: I post on LinkedIn about B2B marketing challenges. Marketers see my posts. They think 'this guy gets it.' They check my profile. They see Exit Five. They join. Inside the community, they get value. They share what they learned on LinkedIn. Their connections see it. And the cycle repeats."

"This is a LOOP, not a funnel. LinkedIn creates the awareness. The community creates the retention. The members create more LinkedIn content. It feeds itself."

**On monetization through organic:**
"We monetize through membership, sponsorships, and events. All of it traced back to LinkedIn organic content. Our sponsors come to us because they see our LinkedIn presence. They say 'we see your posts everywhere — how do we get in front of your audience?' We never cold-pitch sponsors. They come inbound."

**On the importance of consistency:**
"The secret isn't talent. It's not that I'm a better writer than anyone else. The secret is that I've posted on LinkedIn almost every business day for 6 years. That's ~1,500 posts. Most people give up after 30 posts and say 'LinkedIn doesn't work for me.' LinkedIn works. You just quit too early."

### Key Themes
- Exit Five grew from 0 to 5,000+ paying members entirely through LinkedIn organic content
- The content-community loop: LinkedIn → awareness → community → retention → member content → LinkedIn
- Sponsors come inbound because of organic LinkedIn presence
- Consistency over 6+ years (~1,500 posts) is the real "secret"
