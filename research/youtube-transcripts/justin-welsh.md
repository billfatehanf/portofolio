## Video 1: "How Justin Welsh Built a $5M Solo Business on LinkedIn"

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| **Channel** | The Knowledge Project (Shane Parrish) |

| **Date** | October 2025 |
| **Duration** | ~55 min |
| **Speaker** | Justin Welsh |

### Transcript Summary

Welsh opens by establishing his credibility through practice:

"I want to be clear — I'm not a LinkedIn guru. I'm a guy who used LinkedIn to build a business. The business came first. The 'expertise' on LinkedIn came from doing it every day for four years and seeing what worked."

**On the origin of his content system:**
"In 2019, I was burned out from running sales teams in SaaS. I left my VP role, had no idea what to do next, and started posting on LinkedIn because it was free. I had zero followers, zero audience, zero plan."

"The first 90 days were brutal. I'd post something and get 3 likes — two from my mom and one from my old colleague. But I kept going because I had nothing else. And slowly, around month 4, things started compounding. Posts went from 200 views to 2,000 to 20,000."

**On the 'Think Once, Publish 10 Times' method:**
"People ask me how I post 5-7 times per week without burning out. The answer: I don't create 5-7 pieces of content. I create ONE deep piece — my Saturday newsletter — and then I extract 5-7 ideas from it."

"Let me give you a concrete example. Last week I wrote a newsletter about why most solopreneurs fail in their first year. From that one essay, I created:
1. A listicle: '7 reasons solopreneurs fail in year one'
2. A story post about my own near-failure
3. A contrarian take: 'Failure isn't a badge of honor — it's a sign you didn't have a system'
4. An observation about patterns I see in my community
5. A 'here's what I'd do differently' post
6. A comparison: 'What losing solopreneurs do vs winning solopreneurs'

Six posts from one newsletter. That's 2 hours of deep thinking turning into a full week of content."

**On profile optimization:**
"Your LinkedIn profile has ONE job: make people follow you or click your CTA. That's it. Everything else is vanity."

"The formula I use is: WHO + WHAT + HOW. 'I help solopreneurs build $1M+ businesses using LinkedIn and email.' That's my actual headline. It tells you who I help, what I help them do, and how I do it."

"If your headline says 'VP of Sales at Acme Corp' — you've wasted the most valuable real estate in your professional life."

**On the 'Draft Off Giants' strategy:**
"This was my secret weapon in the early days. I identified 10 creators with huge audiences — people like Gary Vee, Sahil Bloom, Lenny Rachitsky. I turned on notifications for each of them. When they posted, I'd drop a thoughtful comment within 15 minutes."

"Not 'Great post!' — that's worthless. Something like: 'This resonates. I found that when I applied this principle to my newsletter, open rates went from 32% to 47% in 3 months. The key insight for me was...' — genuine value that makes people click on YOUR profile."

"I estimate 40% of my first 50k followers came from strategic commenting. It's the most underrated growth hack on LinkedIn."

**On monetization:**
"I monetize through two digital products: The Operating System ($150) and The Content OS ($150). Together they've done over $5M in revenue. No employees, no investors, no office."

"LinkedIn drives newsletter subscribers. The newsletter builds trust. The trust converts to product sales. That's the entire business model."

### Key Themes
- Built $5M+ solo business from zero LinkedIn followers through consistent posting
- "Think Once, Publish 10 Times" — one newsletter = 5-7 LinkedIn posts
- Profile formula: WHO + WHAT + HOW
- "Draft Off Giants" — strategic commenting drives 40% of early growth
- LinkedIn → Newsletter → Digital products = complete solo business model

## Video 2: "The LinkedIn Content Strategy That Actually Works in 2026"

| Field | Detail |
|-------|--------|

| **Date** | January 2026 |
| **Duration** | ~42 min |
| **Speaker** | Justin Welsh |

### Transcript Summary

In conversation with Ali Abdaal, Welsh discusses what's changed on LinkedIn:

"LinkedIn in 2026 is very different from 2020. The platform is saturated with AI-generated content. ChatGPT made it easy for anyone to pump out '10 tips for success' posts. So the bar has risen dramatically."

**On standing out in the AI era:**
"The way to stand out now is what I call 'unique knowledge.' This is insight that ONLY you can share because it comes from YOUR specific experience. AI can't replicate it because AI hasn't lived your life."

"For example, I can share: 'In my first 90 days of solopreneurship, I made $0. I ate ramen, panicked every night, and almost went back to corporate. Here's the ONE thing that changed everything.' — no AI can generate that. It's authentically mine."

**On the 'Spiky Point of View':**
"Every creator who succeeds on LinkedIn has what I call a 'spiky point of view.' It's an opinion so specific and bold that it polarizes people. Some love it, some hate it. But nobody is indifferent."

"Mine is: 'You don't need a team, funding, or ads to build a million-dollar business.' That's spiky. It challenges conventional wisdom. It attracts solopreneurs who believe the same. It repels people who think I'm wrong. Both reactions are valuable."

"If your content doesn't make SOMEONE uncomfortable, it's too safe."

**On doing less, better:**
"I used to think I needed to be on every platform. LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube, TikTok. I was spreading myself thin and growing nowhere."

"In 2021, I made a decision: LinkedIn only. I went ALL IN on one platform. Within 6 months, my growth rate doubled. Because all my energy, all my experimentation, all my learning was focused on one algorithm, one audience, one format."

"My advice to anyone starting: pick ONE platform and master it. Don't spread yourself thin. You can expand later. But mastery of one platform beats mediocrity across five."

### Key Themes
- AI saturation has raised the bar; "unique knowledge" from personal experience is the differentiator
- "Spiky Point of View" — bold, specific opinions that polarize attract the right audience
- Going all-in on ONE platform (LinkedIn) doubled growth rate in 6 months
- Authentic, experience-based storytelling is the antidote to AI-generated content
