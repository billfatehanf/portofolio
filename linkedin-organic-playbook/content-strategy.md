# Content Strategy

The fundamental content principles the 10 experts leverage to command outsized reach:

### 1. Zero-Click Philosophy
LinkedIn's algorithm is designed to keep users on the platform. Outbound links suppress reach by up to 50%.
* **Strategy:** Deliver the *entire* essay, framework, or lesson directly in the text body or image/carousel. 
* *(Source: [Justin Welsh](../research/sources.md), [`../research/linkedin-posts/justin-welsh.md`, Post 5, January 2026])*

### 2. High-Specificity Formatting
Generic platitudes blend into the feed. Specificity demands attention.
* **Strategy:** Build "Before & After" teardowns. Use explicit data points (e.g., "From 5% to 15% conversion"). Frame content around specific, painful use-cases.
* *(Source: [Anthony Pierri](../research/sources.md), [`../research/youtube-transcripts/anthony-pierri.md`, Video 2: "Finding the Right Messaging and Positioning"])*

### 3. The Central Content Engine
Relying entirely on off-the-cuff inspiration for LinkedIn is unsustainable.
* **Strategy:** Power your short-form LinkedIn posts with a long-form native content engine (a weekly podcast, newsletter, or deep-dive technical blog).
* *(Source: [Chris Walker](../research/sources.md), [`../research/youtube-transcripts/chris-walker.md`, Video 2: "Evidence-Based Medicine"])*
